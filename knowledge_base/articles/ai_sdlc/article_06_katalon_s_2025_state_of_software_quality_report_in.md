# Katalon's 2025 State of Software Quality Report: Insights from 1,500 QA Professionals Worldwide

## Overview

Katalon released its 2025 State of Software Quality Report, surveying over 1,500 quality professionals across North America, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. The report examines how QA teams are evolving from execution-focused roles to strategic business drivers, and how AI adoption is reshaping the profession—both in practice and in perception.

## Survey Methodology

The report is based on responses from more than 1,500 QA professionals worldwide, spanning roles from individual testers to QA managers and directors. Respondents were drawn from multiple industries and represent organizations at varying levels of testing maturity.

## Headline Statistics

- **76%** of QA professionals now use AI-powered tools in their testing workflows
- **72%** leverage AI specifically for test generation and script optimization
- **82%** affirm that AI skills will be critical to the future of QA within the next 3–5 years
- **56%** of teams still struggle to keep pace with testing demands despite AI adoption
- **Only 11%** of teams have reached an optimized maturity level using advanced automation or AI
- **77%** of respondents say aligning QA with business goals has helped improve customer retention
- **48%** of organizations now view QA as a competitive advantage

## The AI Paradox in QA

Despite widespread fears of job displacement, QA professionals are rapidly adopting AI tools. Testers using AI are **twice as likely** to fear replacement—yet those with stronger AI fluency are testing faster and reclaiming satisfaction in their roles. This contradiction reveals a persistent gap between enthusiasm for AI and the realized outcomes it produces.

The data surfaces a clear adoption-versus-outcomes gap: while the majority of teams have introduced AI tooling, only a small fraction have reached the optimized maturity level where those tools drive measurable efficiency gains at scale. The majority of teams remain in earlier adoption stages—experimenting or integrating AI without yet optimizing processes around it.

**Key AI adoption statistics:**
- 61% of QA teams are adopting AI-driven testing practices
- 53% of teams report actively adopting AI-driven approaches
- 67% of teams are investing in continuous learning to build AI fluency

## Maturity Levels: The Adoption-Outcomes Gap

The report segments QA teams by maturity level, from ad hoc and manual-first teams through to optimized, AI-augmented organizations. The distribution reveals a sharp pyramid:

- The **majority** of teams remain at early or developing maturity stages
- Only **11%** have reached an optimized level with advanced automation or AI embedded in their workflows
- **56%** report that they still cannot keep pace with testing demands—even as AI adoption rates climb

This gap between high adoption intent and low maturity outcomes suggests that having AI tools is not the same as having AI-enabled processes. Infrastructure gaps, skill shortages, and organizational inertia slow the translation from tool adoption to productivity gains.

## AI Integration with Human Expertise: The Hybrid Testing Model

High-maturity QA teams leverage a hybrid model combining AI, automation, and manual testing skills. These teams demonstrate measurably superior performance:

- **1.3x more likely** to adopt AI-augmented test optimization
- **1.8x more likely** to implement intelligent test maintenance practices

The report argues that the future of QA is not AI replacing testers, but hybrid testers who orchestrate AI tools, interpret results, and apply domain knowledge where automation cannot reach.

## The Happiness Edge: Job Satisfaction and Performance

The report highlights a strong correlation between QA professional satisfaction and team performance outcomes. Satisfied QA professionals show measurable advantages over their less satisfied peers:

- **1.4x more likely** to implement advanced automation solutions (46% vs. 34%)
- **1.4x more likely** to report AI-improved efficiency (71% vs. 52%)

This finding underscores that investment in people—through upskilling, tooling, and autonomy—pays dividends in quality outcomes. Teams that treat satisfaction as a performance variable outperform those that do not.

## AI-Driven Testing Adoption Patterns

The report breaks down how teams are currently applying AI within their testing practices:

- **61%** use AI to automate repetitive tasks (regression, smoke, data generation)
- **72%** specifically use AI for test generation and script optimization
- **34%** are investing in performance and load testing tools
- **30%** are implementing dedicated test management platforms

## Modernization Priorities for High-Performing Teams

High-performing teams are focusing their modernization efforts on three critical areas:

1. **AI-driven tools** — 61% of top teams are adopting AI-assisted testing
2. **Modern development practices** — 51% are aligning QA to agile, CI/CD, and DevOps workflows
3. **Continuous testing** — 40% are investing in pipeline-embedded, always-on test execution

## Strategic Implications: QA as Business Enabler

The report documents a shift in how organizations perceive QA. Quality assurance is transitioning from a defensive safeguard—catching bugs before release—to a strategic business enabler that drives competitive differentiation.

- 77% of respondents say aligning QA with business goals has improved customer retention
- 48% of organizations now view QA as a competitive advantage
- Teams combining AI fluency with human insight are positioned as innovation drivers, not just defect detectors

## Key Takeaways for QA Leaders

1. **AI adoption is broad but shallow.** Three-quarters of teams use AI tools, but only 11% have optimized their processes around them. The gap is process and skill, not tooling availability.
2. **Hybrid skills are the differentiator.** Teams that invest in building testers who can work alongside AI—not just use it—achieve 1.3–1.8x better outcomes on key automation metrics.
3. **Satisfaction drives performance.** Happier QA professionals deliver measurably better automation and AI adoption outcomes. Investment in team wellbeing is a quality investment.
4. **Alignment with business goals matters.** QA teams that speak the language of business outcomes—retention, revenue risk, competitive advantage—are more likely to receive investment and achieve strategic influence.
5. **The demand-capacity gap persists.** 56% of teams cannot keep pace with testing demands. AI adoption alone does not close this gap; it requires process redesign and organizational commitment.

## Conclusion

Katalon's 2025 report paints a picture of a profession at an inflection point. AI tools are nearly ubiquitous in QA, yet the majority of teams have not yet translated adoption into optimized outcomes. The path forward requires moving beyond tool acquisition to building hybrid human-AI workflows, investing in practitioner skills, and positioning QA as a strategic driver of business quality—not just a technical gatekeeper.
