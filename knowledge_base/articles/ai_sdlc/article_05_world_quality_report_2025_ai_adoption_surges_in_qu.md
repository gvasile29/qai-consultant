# World Quality Report 2025: AI Adoption Surges in Quality Engineering, but Enterprise-Level Scaling Remains Elusive

**Published:** November 13, 2025
**Source:** OpenText, Capgemini, and Sogeti — 17th Edition of the World Quality Report

The World Quality Report 2025 reveals a significant gap between organizational interest in generative AI for quality engineering and actual implementation capability. The annual survey covers 1,800+ organizations across 30 countries.

## Key Findings

### Widespread Adoption but Limited Scale

- 89% of organizations are piloting or deploying Gen AI-augmented workflows
- 37% have implementations in production; 52% remain in pilot phases
- Only 15% achieved enterprise-wide implementation
- 43% remain in experimental phases; 30% operate within limited use cases

### Shifting Non-Adoption Trends

Non-adopters increased to 11% in 2025 from 4% in 2024, though this remains significantly lower than 2023's 31%, suggesting organizations are moving from rapid experimentation to more deliberate, strategy-driven approaches.

### Use Case Evolution

Generative AI deployment is shifting from analyzing outputs (defect analysis, reporting) toward shaping inputs, with test case design and requirements refinement now leading adoption patterns. Synthetic data generation has emerged as the top GenAI use case, surging from 14% to 25% adoption year-over-year.

### Productivity Gains with Caveats

Organizations report an average 19% productivity boost, yet one-third experienced minimal gains, underscoring the necessity for more sophisticated integration methodologies.

### Top Barriers to Enterprise Scaling

- Data privacy risks: 67%
- Integration complexity: 64%
- Hallucination and reliability concerns: 60%
- Skills gap: 50%

These barriers represent a shift from 2024's more strategic obstacles (validation strategy gaps, insufficient skills, unclear organizational structure).

## Expert Perspectives

**Tal Levi-Joseph**, Senior Vice President of Application Delivery Management at OpenText, emphasized that "quality engineering is being redefined by AI" and that remaining stationary is untenable for competitive organizations.

**Mark Buenen**, Global Leader of Quality Engineering & Testing at Capgemini, noted the transition from early experimentation to strategic integration, highlighting persistent struggles with aligning AI-enabled quality engineering to business objectives.

## Strategic Implications

Many organizations treat generative AI as tactical enhancement rather than strategic transformation, resulting in fragmented execution and underfunded programs. Success requires investment in:

- Skills development
- Governance frameworks
- Data management
- Outcome alignment

The report emphasizes **"collaborative intelligence"**—combining human expertise with AI capabilities—as essential for navigating the tension between innovation and accountability. Shift-left approaches remain dominant, though shift-right methodologies are gaining momentum.

Human expertise cannot be substituted by AI; instead, AI amplifies existing capabilities in design, development, and testing when quality engineering fundamentals are strengthened first.

## QAI Consultant Application

This report provides authoritative, data-backed context for QA strategy recommendations:

- **Baseline for GenAI maturity:** Only 15% enterprise-scale adoption is the industry baseline; projects claiming higher readiness should be validated rigorously.
- **Risk framing:** Data privacy risks (67%) and hallucination/reliability concerns (60%) are the leading blockers — these should be surfaced in Risk Registers for any AI-augmented QA initiative.
- **Effort calibration:** The 19% average productivity gain and the one-third experiencing minimal gains justify moderate (not aggressive) efficiency multipliers when estimating effort for AI-assisted testing.
- **Use case prioritization:** Synthetic data generation (25% adoption, up from 14%) and test case design are the highest-ROI GenAI use cases to recommend in Test Strategies.
- **Scaling risk:** Projects planning enterprise-wide GenAI QA rollout should include governance, skills development, and data management workstreams in their Test Strategy — not just tooling adoption.
