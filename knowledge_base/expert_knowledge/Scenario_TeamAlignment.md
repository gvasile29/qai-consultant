# Real Scenario — Aligning Teams on an Embedded Automotive Project

**Contributor:** Gabi  
**Date:** February 2026  
**Experience context:** Experienced QA professional in automotive software, involved with both embedded systems and mobile application teams, managing cross-team alignment challenges.

---

## The Scenario

An automotive project involved two teams: one working on the embedded system (installed on a motorbike) and one working on the mobile application. The challenge was that the two teams did **not have a common understanding** of the embedded product, which risked misaligned development and integration issues.

---

## First Instinct

Concern — it was clear that the teams were not speaking the same language and their understanding of the product differed.

---

## What I Did — Step by Step

1. Raised my concerns with the **project manager** and **software architect**, who confirmed the issue.  
2. Organized an initial **alignment meeting** with both teams to establish a shared understanding of the current state.  
3. Set up a **live task force in a war-room**, where both teams worked physically together to foster real-time communication and human interaction.  

---

## What Worked

- Bringing everyone together **ensured everyone understood the final goals of the project**.  
- The hands-on, collaborative environment helped clarify ambiguities and avoid miscommunication.  

---

## What I Would Do Differently

- Start a **task force at the beginning of the project**, putting both teams together to establish a common mindset from day one.  
- Proactively define shared goals and expectations to avoid future misalignment.  

---

## The Key Insight

**Speak it out loud!** There is no problem in raising concerns or pointing out potential risks early.  

---

## How to Recognize This Scenario

- Teams frequently **misinterpret product requirements** or deliverables.  
- Communication between teams is **inconsistent or conflicting**.  
- Early signs of **integration or interface misunderstandings** appear during development.  

---

## QAI Consultant Should...

- Alert the QA lead or project manager about potential **team misalignment**.  
- Suggest **joint alignment sessions** or “war-room” workshops.  
- Recommend creation of **shared documentation**, diagrams, or visual aids.  
- Monitor communications for **conflicting specifications or misunderstandings** and flag risks early.
